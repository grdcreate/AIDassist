Pack Your Surplus Food

Proper packaging ensures food safety and quality during transportation. Follow our guidelines to make your donation count.

Food Packaging Guide

Learn how to properly package different types of food for donation

Choose Appropriate Containers

Use clean, food-grade containers with tight-fitting lids to prevent spills and contamination.

Maintain Safe Temperatures

Keep hot foods hot (>60°C) and cold foods cold (<5°C) until packaging and pickup.

Label Clearly

Mark containers with food type, preparation date, and any allergens to ensure safe handling.

Step-by-Step Packaging

Follow these simple steps to package your surplus food safely

1

Wash Hands and Surfaces

Start with clean hands, utensils, and packaging materials to prevent contamination.

2

Separate Different Foods

Package different food types separately to maintain quality and prevent cross-contamination.

3

Use Appropriate Containers

Select containers that are the right size for the quantity of food, leaving some headspace for expansion.

4

Seal Tightly

Ensure containers are securely closed to prevent leaks and maintain freshness during transport.

5

Label Clearly

Include food name, preparation date, and any special instructions or allergens on the container.

6

Keep at Safe Temperature

Store packaged food appropriately until pickup - refrigerated or frozen as needed.

Food Type Guidelines

Specific packaging recommendations for different food categories

Prepared Meals

  • Package in individual portions when possible
  • Use microwave-safe containers if applicable
  • Separate sauces and gravies from solid components
  • Cool completely before sealing and refrigerating

Bakery Items

  • Package in breathable containers to prevent sogginess
  • Separate different types to maintain freshness
  • Label with type and any allergens (nuts, gluten, etc.)
  • Freeze if not being consumed within 2 days

Fresh Produce

  • Wash and dry before packaging
  • Use ventilated containers for longer freshness
  • Separate ethylene-producing fruits from others
  • Package delicate items to prevent bruising

Food Safety First

Essential safety practices to ensure donated food is safe for consumption

Time Limits

Donate food within safe timeframes - typically within 2 hours of preparation

Temperature Control

Maintain proper temperatures during storage and transportation

Allergen Awareness

Clearly label common allergens like nuts, dairy, gluten, and shellfish

What Not to Donate

Avoid donating food that has been partially consumed or is past its expiry date

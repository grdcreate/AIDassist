Food Safety Guidelines

Ensuring safe food handling practices for donors and recipients

Essential Food Safety Practices

Follow these guidelines to ensure the food you donate is safe and nutritious

Cleanliness

Maintain proper hygiene when handling food:

  • Wash hands thoroughly with soap and water
  • Clean and sanitize all surfaces and equipment
  • Rinse fruits and vegetables under running water
  • Use clean containers and packaging

Temperature Control

Proper temperature control prevents bacterial growth:

  • Keep hot foods hot (above 140°F/60°C)
  • Keep cold foods cold (below 40°F/4°C)
  • Use insulated containers for transport
  • Discard perishable food left out for more than 2 hours

Proper Packaging

Ensure food is properly packaged and labeled:

  • Use airtight containers to prevent contamination
  • Label packages with contents and date
  • Separate raw and cooked foods during transport
  • Use appropriate packaging for different food types

Safe Food Handling

Follow these practices to prevent cross-contamination:

  • Use separate cutting boards for raw meat and produce
  • Avoid touching ready-to-eat foods with bare hands
  • Store raw meat below other foods in refrigeration
  • Clean spills immediately to prevent bacterial spread

Timely Donation

Donate food while it's still fresh and safe to consume:

  • Donate perishable items before expiration dates
  • Freeze items if you can't donate within 1-2 days
  • Check for signs of spoilage before donating
  • Coordinate with food banks for timely pickup/delivery

What Not to Donate

Avoid donating these items for safety reasons:

  • Foods with signs of mold or spoilage
  • Foods from damaged or swollen cans
  • Foods that have been recalled
  • Home-canned foods (unless from approved sources)
  • Foods past their expiration date

Food Temperature Guide

Safe temperature ranges for different food types

Food Type Safe Temperature Range Important Notes
Poultry 165°F (74°C) or higher Cook thoroughly until no pink remains
Ground Meat 160°F (71°C) or higher Ensure even cooking throughout
Fresh Beef, Pork, Lamb 145°F (63°C) with 3-minute rest Use a food thermometer to verify
Fish & Seafood 145°F (63°C) or until opaque Fish should flake easily with a fork
Eggs & Egg Dishes 160°F (71°C) Cook until yolk and white are firm
Leftovers & Casseroles 165°F (74°C) Reheat thoroughly before serving

Following these food safety guidelines helps ensure that donated food is nutritious and safe for those in need.

For more information, consult the FDA Food Safety Guidelines or contact your local health department.
